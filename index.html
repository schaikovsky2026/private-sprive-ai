Approach

Discipline before conviction.

The strategy is a defined, repeatable process — not a discretionary view on the market. Four principles govern every decision.

01

Academic foundation

The selection signal is Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum — the factor formalized in academic finance since 1993, used by AQR, Robeco, and most institutional momentum portfolios. The ranking is rules-based and survives 30+ years of out-of-sample literature.

02

Evidence over intuition

The strategy was refined in May 2026 after systematic factor backtesting showed three of the five original trend filters were actively reducing return without protecting drawdown. They were removed. The decision was made on evidence, not on conviction.

03

Disciplined exit

Positions are held one month and rebalanced on the first Monday. Material thesis breaks — guidance miss, fraud allegation, named-customer loss — trigger an automatic mid-month exit, with the freed allocation held in cash until next rebalance.

04

Transparency as method

Every pick is published. Every losing year is disclosed in full. The backtest deliberately uses a stripped-down version of the strategy (price filters only) to avoid presenting a flattering picture; the live strategy adds quality filters described below.

Selection in two layers

The 18-year backtest below measures the mechanical signal alone — Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum applied to the S&P 500. The live strategy adds a qualitative overlay: each top candidate is researched and scored across six dimensions, with a downside-risk gate excluding capital-loss outliers. The top five by composite score become the month's picks.

Critical methodology disclosure

The numbers above test the strategy on the full US-listed universe (~4,300 tickers) with only price-based filters (price > $5, volume > 1M shares, positive 12-month momentum). This is what yfinance.screen() literally returns. The live strategy in production applies four additional fundamental quality filters — analyst rating, positive trailing EPS, market cap ≥ $2B, forward EPS > trailing EPS — that this backtest cannot replicate historically because yfinance does not expose point-in-time fundamental data. Those filters typically reduce the candidate pool from ~4,300 tickers to ~700. A separate test on the curated S&P 500 universe (a reasonable proxy for the live quality-filtered pool) produced +34.4%/yr / Sharpe 0.92 / Max DD −56.4%. Realistic live performance is expected to be materially above this expanded-universe backtest, but the exact uplift cannot be quantified without point-in-time fundamentals.

On the −71.8% drawdown

Without quality filters, the 12-1 momentum signal picks up junk names that pop and crash. The 2008 crisis took these to the cleaners. Real-world live trading with the full filter set should produce shallower drawdowns. The site uses a realistic T+1 execution model — trades placed the day after the decision, not at the close of the decision day, since no investor can act on a price they have not yet seen.

Risk Disclosure

What you should know.

  • This is research and educational signals — not registered investment advice in Thailand, the United States, or any other jurisdiction.
  • The strategy was refined in May 2026 after systematic factor backtesting. The previous configuration (60-day/20-day momentum with a five-filter screener) was replaced with academic Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum and a tradable-floor filter only. Both configurations are documented; the change was made on evidence, not on conviction.
  • The backtest uses a T+1 execution model — the strategy decides at the close of day T (after market) and executes trades at the close of day T+1 (next trading day). This is realistic for a retail trader who sees the close after-hours and cannot trade at a price not yet seen.
  • The backtest uses the full US-listed universe (~4,300 tickers) from yfinance.screen() but with only price-based filters applied (price > $5, volume > 1M, positive 12-month momentum). The live strategy applies four additional fundamental filters — analyst rating ≥ Hold, positive trailing EPS, market cap ≥ $2B, forward EPS > trailing EPS — that the backtest cannot replicate because yfinance does not expose point-in-time fundamental data. Those filters typically reduce the candidate pool from ~4,300 to ~700.
  • The backtest carries survivorship-bias inflation. The test only uses companies currently listed on US exchanges, so companies that delisted during the test period (Lehman Brothers, WaMu, Bear Stearns, and many others) are silently excluded. Estimated at 2–4% per year.
  • A realistic forward expectation for the live strategy is +10 to +25% annualized IRR. This range reflects (a) the +8% bare backtest as a lower anchor, (b) substantial expected uplift from the live strategy's quality filters (a separate S&P 500 proxy test produced +34% but is itself biased), and (c) survivorship-bias correction. The range is wider than the previous +20–30% claim to reflect honest uncertainty about how much the unmeasurable quality filters add.
  • The strategy lost −53.4% in 2008 on the unfiltered backtest and showed an 18-year maximum drawdown of −71.8%. With live quality filters those numbers should be substantially shallower, but momentum strategies are inherently exposed to deep drawdowns in financial-crisis regimes. Plan for −30% to −50% drawdowns as realistic, not exceptional.
  • The strategy lagged the index in fast-correction regimes (2015–16 China devaluation, 2018 Q4 sell-off, 2009 momentum-crash rally). Leadership-rotation drawdowns are a structural risk of the 12-month signal.
  • A concentrated five-position portfolio is materially more volatile than an index fund. Single-week liquidation events can produce double-digit losses.
  • You retain full responsibility for trading decisions. No returns are guaranteed; the subscription fee is not refundable based on performance.
  • Foreign capital-gains treatment for Thai residents depends on remittance. Consult a Thai accountant before relying on after-tax projections.
  • The live track record on the current strategy is not yet established. The first monthly cycle on the new configuration runs June 2026. The backtest is the primary evidence base — and an inherently limited one.
