Approach

Discipline before conviction.

The strategy is a defined, repeatable process — not a discretionary view on the market. Four principles govern every decision.

01

Academic foundation

The selection signal is Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum — the factor formalized in academic finance since 1993, used by AQR, Robeco, and most institutional momentum portfolios. The ranking is rules-based and survives 30+ years of out-of-sample literature.

02

Evidence over intuition

The strategy was refined in May 2026 after systematic factor backtesting showed three of the five original trend filters were actively reducing return without protecting drawdown. They were removed. The decision was made on evidence, not on conviction.

03

Disciplined exit

Positions are held one month and rebalanced on the first Monday. Material thesis breaks — guidance miss, fraud allegation, named-customer loss — trigger an automatic mid-month exit, with the freed allocation held in cash until next rebalance.

04

Transparency as method

Every pick is published. Every losing year — including the −36.6% return in 2008 — is disclosed. A track record is only useful if it cannot be quietly revised after the fact.

Selection in two layers

The 18-year backtest below measures the mechanical signal alone — Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum applied to the S&P 500. The live strategy adds a qualitative overlay: each top candidate is researched and scored across six dimensions, with a downside-risk gate excluding capital-loss outliers. The top five by composite score become the month's picks.

Risk Disclosure

What you should know.

  • This is research and educational signals — not registered investment advice in Thailand, the United States, or any other jurisdiction.
  • The strategy was refined in May 2026 after systematic factor backtesting. The previous configuration (60-day/20-day momentum with a five-filter screener) was replaced with academic Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum and a tradable-floor filter only. Both configurations are documented; the change was made on evidence, not on conviction.
  • The backtest carries survivorship-bias inflation. Plain meaning: the test only uses companies that are in the S&P 500 today, so companies that went bankrupt during the test period (Lehman Brothers, WaMu, Bear Stearns, GM, Chrysler, and dozens more) are silently excluded. The strategy never had to "buy" those — but a real investor running it in 2008 would have. The inflation is estimated at 3–5% per year over the 18-year window. A realistic forward expectation is +20 to +30% annualized alpha versus the S&P 500, not the +23.8% the backtest shows.
  • The strategy lost −36.6% in 2008 and showed an 18-year maximum drawdown of −57.6%. Momentum strategies are particularly exposed to deep, slow-recovering drawdowns in financial-crisis regimes. Drawdowns of −30% to −50% are plausible in any year and should be planned for, not treated as exceptional.
  • The strategy lagged the index in fast-correction regimes (2015–16 China devaluation, 2018 Q4 sell-off, 2009 momentum-crash rally). Leadership-rotation drawdowns are a structural risk of the 12-month signal.
  • A concentrated five-position portfolio is materially more volatile than an index fund. Single-week liquidation events can produce double-digit losses.
  • You retain full responsibility for trading decisions. No returns are guaranteed; the subscription fee is not refundable based on performance.
  • Foreign capital-gains treatment for Thai residents depends on remittance. Consult a Thai accountant before relying on after-tax projections.
  • The live track record on the current strategy is not yet established. The first monthly cycle on the new configuration runs June 2026. The backtest is the primary evidence base — and an inherently limited one.
  • Past performance does not guarantee future results.
