Approach

Discipline before conviction.

The strategy is a defined, repeatable process — not a discretionary view on the market. Four principles govern every decision.

01

Academic foundation

The selection signal is Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum — the factor formalized in academic finance since 1993, used by AQR, Robeco, and most institutional momentum portfolios. The ranking is rules-based and survives 30+ years of out-of-sample literature.

02

Evidence over intuition

The strategy was refined in May 2026 after systematic factor backtesting showed three of the five original trend filters were actively reducing return without protecting drawdown. They were removed. The decision was made on evidence, not on conviction.

03

Disciplined exit

Positions are held one month and rebalanced on the first Monday. Material thesis breaks — guidance miss, fraud allegation, named-customer loss — trigger an automatic mid-month exit, with the freed allocation held in cash until next rebalance.

04

Transparency as method

Every pick is published. Every losing year — including the −36.6% return in 2008 — is disclosed. The backtest's survivorship-bias inflation is quantified, not hidden. A track record is only useful if it cannot be quietly revised after the fact.

From signal to selection

Two layers, one strategy.

The backtest measures the mechanical signal alone. The live strategy adds a qualitative research layer on top — the work that turns a ranked list into a decision.

Layer 1 · Mechanical

The signal (backtested)

  • S&P 500 universe
  • Jegadeesh-Titman 12-1 momentum ranking
  • Tradable-floor screen plus quality gates
  • Produces ~15–30 ranked candidates each month

This is what the 18-year backtest validated.

Layer 2 · Qualitative

The research (overlay)

  • Each top candidate is researched across six dimensions
  • Each dimension is scored 1–10, then weighted
  • A downside-risk gate excludes capital-loss outliers
  • The top five by composite score become that month's picks

This is what the engagement covers.

The six dimensions

DimensionWeightWhat it measures
Catalyst proximity & quality20%Near-term price-moving event — earnings beat, raised guidance, product launch, contract win, regulatory ruling. Higher score = sooner and more material.
Revenue growth acceleration20%Quarter-over-quarter growth trajectory — whether the rate is speeding up, not just whether absolute growth is high.
Momentum quality20%Strength of the 12-1 signal combined with volume confirmation and relative strength versus the S&P 500.
Sentiment quality15%Broad multi-source check: financial news, analyst tone, earnings-call language, social and retail sentiment, institutional commentary.
Continuation probability15%Is the move sustainable? Sector tailwind, institutional flow, room versus analyst price target, position not over-crowded.
Fundamental quality10%Margin expansion, Rule of 40 for software companies, operating cash flow, defensible competitive moat.

Plus a mandatory downside-risk gate: any candidate carrying material capital-loss risk — fraud allegation, going-concern issue, broken thesis — is excluded from selection regardless of its composite score. Score weights are not fixed; they will be re-evaluated against alpha contribution once thirty closed positions have accumulated.

Honest note on backtest versus live

The backtest above measures Layer 1 in isolation — what the mechanical signal alone would have produced. The qualitative overlay in Layer 2 may add value (avoiding fraud risk, timing catalysts), be approximately neutral, or modestly subtract value (over-thinking, missing winners). Which one is true will not be known until thirty or more closed positions have accumulated. The realistic forward expectation of +20 to +30%/yr already builds in a margin for this uncertainty.

On the 2009 momentum crash

March–May 2009 is the canonical "momentum crash" — the most-beaten-down stocks of 2008 rallied hardest as the recession bottomed, and momentum portfolios that owned 2008's leaders gave back relative ground. The strategy returned +9.2% during that window while the index returned +28.8%. The strategy did not lose money, but it materially lagged. This pattern recurs whenever leadership rotates faster than the 12-month signal updates (also visible in 2015–16 and 2018 Q4).

On the 2023–2025 numbers

The +125%, +189%, +108% recent years are in-sample, regime-favorable, and contain heavy survivorship-bias inflation (the backtest uses current S&P 500 constituents; bankrupt companies are excluded retroactively). These numbers should be discounted heavily. The 18-year average of +35%/yr is more representative; the realistic forward expectation of +20 to +30%/yr is more conservative still.
